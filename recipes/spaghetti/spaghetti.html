Easy Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is the ultimate easy-cooking comfort food. Follow this recipe for perfecting the simple yet rich dish and its trademark silky, smooth sauce.

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1/2 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese (2 ounces), plus more for garnish
  • 6 ounces pancetta, finely diced
  • 12 ounces spaghetti

Steps

  1. Whisk together eggs and Pecorino Romano in a medium bowl.
  2. Cook pancetta in a large skillet over medium heat, stirring often, until evenly browned, about 10 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti according to package directions, reserving 23 cup pasta cooking water. Drain; add spaghetti to pancetta in skillet. Remove skillet from heat.
  4. Stir 1⁄4 cup pasta water into egg mixture to temper eggs. Add egg mixture to spaghetti in skillet; stir constantly, adding enough pasta cooking water to reach desired consistency, until spaghetti is coated and eggs are just set but not curdled.
  5. Pecorino Romano is aged sheep’s-milk cheese with a sharp and salty flavor. For the best texture, grate your own. But you can substitute Parmesan, no problem.
